## When a commercial license is required

You need a separate commercial license if **either** of the following applies:

1. **Non-copyleft use** — You want to use this software without AGPL-3.0's copyleft obligations (for example, in a closed-source, proprietary, or SaaS product where you do not disclose modifications under AGPL).

2. **Revenue threshold** — You use this software commercially and expect **gross revenue over USD $10,000** from products or services that incorporate or depend on this software.

If neither condition applies, you may use the software under [AGPL-3.0](LICENSE) at no charge, subject to its terms.

## What a commercial license provides

Subject to a signed agreement and applicable fees, a commercial license typically includes:

- A **non-exclusive** grant to use, modify, and distribute the software (or specified portions) for your agreed use case.
- Relief from AGPL-3.0 **copyleft** requirements for the licensed scope, as defined in the signed agreement.
- Terms negotiated for your distribution model, deployment environment, and scale.

## What is excluded

Unless explicitly agreed in writing, a commercial license does **not** include:

- **Trademarks** — No rights to Selfcrafted names, logos, or branding beyond what AGPL or the signed agreement expressly permits.
- **Warranty** — The software is provided without warranty unless otherwise stated in the signed agreement.
- **Support or maintenance** — No obligation to provide support, updates, or maintenance unless separately agreed.

## How to obtain a commercial license

Email **creo@selfcrafted.in** with:

- Your **company or organization** name
- Your **use case** (how you plan to use the software)
- Your **distribution model** (internal only, SaaS, on-premise, embedded, etc.)
- Your **revenue scale** (current or projected gross revenue related to the use)

We will respond with terms and next steps for a signed commercial license agreement.
